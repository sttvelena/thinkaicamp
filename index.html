Frequently Asked Questions

What prior experience does my child need to join the camp?

No experience is needed. The camp is designed for beginners aged 8 to 12. We'll teach everything from scratch in a fun and simple way.

Why is Syntx.ai required?

Instead of signing up for five different platforms and hitting free-tier limits on each one, your child works inside a single subscription that includes everything: image generation, music, voiceover, text, and more — all in one place.

Here's why it makes sense:

  • • One login, one subscription. No juggling accounts for Midjourney, Suno, ElevenLabs, or Claude separately.
  • • No free-tier limits. Kids can create freely without hitting paywalls mid-lesson.
  • • Saves money. Individual subscriptions to these tools would cost significantly more than $18/month combined.
  • • Simple and safe. One parent account, one place, easy to manage.

During the camp we'll also show how individual tools like Midjourney, Claude, Suno, and NanoBanana work on their own, so kids understand the AI landscape. For hands-on project work, we'll use Syntx.ai throughout the week.

You can cancel your Syntx.ai subscription at any time. One month is all you need.

What if my child misses a day?

All sessions are recorded and shared privately with families.

Is it really safe?

Safety is our top priority, and we cover this topic in detail on the very first day of the course. Nevertheless, we recommend that parents periodically take an interest in what their child is doing during lessons and, if necessary, personally monitor the learning process and regularly discuss with their children what they are doing on the computer. We cannot track each child's actions individually, so your involvement is the best guarantee of safety.

Do I need to stay with my child during the online classes?

No, you don't need to. The teacher leads the entire class, and the platform is safe. Parents can drop in anytime, but it's not required.

How many children will be in one group?

We keep groups small, usually between 6 to 10 kids, so the teacher can give personal attention to each child.
